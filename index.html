DSCR Tractor Model Calculation

Excel

Developed a Tractor DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) Financial Model in Excel to assess the loan feasibility of agricultural equipment financing. The model integrates input assumptions such as tractor cost, revenue per acre, daily acres serviced, and operating expenses, and computes annual revenue, expenses, and net operating income. I implemented dynamic DSCR calculations by incorporating loan terms, interest rates, and insurance costs, enabling automated evaluation of loan repayment capacity. The solution features scenario analysis (Best, Moderate, and Worst Case) to test revenue and expense sensitivities, producing DSCR ratios and loan feasibility outcomes in real time. The model delivers clear insights into tractor investment viability, showing a DSCR of 211% under the moderate case, indicating strong loan feasibility, while also highlighting conditions under which repayment becomes risky. This solution demonstrates my expertise in Excel-based financial modeling, scenario planning, and credit risk analysis, with practical application in agribusiness financing.

Data-Driven Insights on AfDB “High 5” Portfolio

Excel, powerbi

This project centers on analyzing the African Development Bank’s (AfDB) High 5 portfolio to uncover insights into funding flows, delivery performance, and regional disparities. Using Excel and Power BI, I conducted a comprehensive analysis, including trend reviews, regional comparisons, and theme-specific evaluations across the five priority areas: Feed Africa, Light Up & Power Africa, Industrialize Africa, Integrate Africa, and Improve the Quality of Life for the People of Africa. Key findings reveal that while AfDB has committed over $81 billion across nearly 6,000 projects, the completion rate stands at 77%, with 941 projects delayed. The analysis also highlights a 49.9% disbursement-to-commitment ratio, suggesting room to accelerate financial execution, and identifies Southern and West Africa as major delay hotspots. At the same time, regions such as East and North Africa emerge as leaders in absorbing funds, particularly within the Quality of Life and Feed Africa priorities. The project serves as an exploration of AfDB’s portfolio performance, offering actionable insights for optimizing disbursement, reducing delays, and strengthening regional balance. By harnessing the power of Excel for data preparation and Power BI for visualization, this initiative provides a structured foundation for monitoring progress, identifying delivery bottlenecks, and informing future investment strategies that advance AfDB’s High 5 goals..

Excelerate Platform Growth and Impact Analysis

Excel, Looker Studio

I led a cross-functional team at Excelerate to analyze user engagement and opportunity participation across 102 countries. We extracted, cleaned, and validated platform data, then performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) on key metrics including sign-ups, demographics, skills gained, opportunity completion rates, and rewards disbursed. Leveraging Excel and Looker Studio, we identified that internships were the most applied-for opportunities and tracked the distribution of over $2.7M in scholarships and stipends. Our findings were delivered through an interactive dashboard and a set of strategic recommendations—expanding digital campaigns, improving follow-up and motivation, and adopting self-paced internship models—designed to enhance growth, optimize engagement, and strengthen the impact of the platform
