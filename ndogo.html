Ripples of Access: Data-Driven Solutions for Water Equity in Maji Ndogo

Using Power BI and SQL for National Water Improvement Across Rural and Urban Communities

As a Data Analyst working on the National Water Improvement Project in Maji Ndogo, I developed an end-to-end analytics solution to track water infrastructure development across rural and urban communities. Leveraging SQL, I cleaned and structured project data, standardizing town names, vendor details, costs, and completion timelines to create a reliable dataset. This dataset was then modeled in Power BI, where I built interactive dashboards to monitor key indicators such as budget performance, cumulative project costs, percentage completion, and population coverage. Using calculated measures and relational models, I identified insights into cost drivers, vendor efficiency, and regional disparities in water access. The analysis revealed which interventions—such as drilling wells, installing taps, and upgrading filtration systems—had the greatest impact on community access at the lowest cost. These insights empowered stakeholders to allocate resources more effectively, ensuring that over 18 million people gained reliable access to clean water. This project highlights my ability to combine SQL-based data preparation with Power BI storytelling to solve critical infrastructure challenges, drive transparency, and inform data-driven policy decisions.
